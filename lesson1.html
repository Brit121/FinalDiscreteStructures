Lesson 1: Discrete Structures

Learning Outcomes:

  • Students will be able to explain what a proposition is.
  • Students will be able to distinguish a proposition from other types of sentences.
  • Students will be able to determine the truth value (true or false) of simple propositions.

WHAT IS A PROPOSITION?

A proposition is a type of statement or claim that is expressed in declarative form and can be answered as either true or false.

WHAT IS A PROPOSITION?

It is a Declarative Statement:

A proposition is a statement that is expressed in declarative form whose primary function is to convey information, state a fact, offer an explanation, or express an opinion.

A sentence stated in the form of a question, request/command, or exclamation is NOT considered a proposition.

Tell whether the following statements are declarative statements or not:

  • The Philippines is a country in Asia.
  • The Philippines is composed of 7,107 islands.
  • 2 √ó 2 = 4.
  • What do you do for fun?
  • Please close the door behind you.
  • Wow!

Tell whether the following statements are declarative statements or not:

  • The Philippines is a country in Asia. (Declarative)
  • The Philippines is composed of 7,107 islands. (Declarative)
  • 2 √ó 2 = 4. (Declarative)
  • What do you do for fun? (Not Declarative ‚Äì Question)
  • Please close the door behind you. (Not Declarative ‚Äì Command)
  • Wow! (Not Declarative ‚Äì Exclamation)

WHAT IS A PROPOSITION?

It has a Truth Value:
A proposition is a definitive statement that is answerable by either true or false but never both or in between.

The truth value is a definite, objective, non-contradictory characteristic of the proposition.

Tell whether the following statements are answerable by a definite true or false.

  • The sky is blue. (Answerable by true or false)
  • 8 √ó 6 = 36. (Answerable by true or false)
  • x + 5 = 25. (Not answerable by true or false)
  • Chocolate is the best flavor of ice cream. (Not answerable by true or false)
  • It will rain tomorrow. (Not answerable by true or false)
  • The weather is nice. (Not answerable by true or false)

WHY SOME STATEMENTS ARE NOT PROPOSITIONS:

  • It is a statement stated as an opinion: Opinions are subjective statements or personal beliefs and are not universally true or false.
  • It is a statement made about the future: While the statement may eventually become true or false, its truth value is unknown at the time of expression.

WHY SOME STATEMENTS ARE NOT PROPOSITIONS:

  • It is an open sentence or a sentence with unknown variables: The truth depends on the value of an unknown variable and is not a proposition until a specific value is assigned.
  • It is a statement with ambiguous terms: Statements with unclear or subjective words depend on personal interpretation, so their truth value is not definite.
