Proposition Classes

Propositions are classified based on their truth values into three categories.

  • Tautology
  • Contradiction
  • Contingency

Tautology

A tautology is a compound proposition that is always true, regardless of the truth values of its individual components.

In other words, no matter what truth values you assign to the variables within the proposition, the entire proposition will always evaluate to true.
